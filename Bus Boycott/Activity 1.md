### **Activity: Document Analysis & Discussion**
**Objective:**  
Students will analyze primary and secondary sources to understand the legal, social, and organizational framework of the Montgomery Bus Boycott and its historical significance.

**Materials:**  
- Excerpts from *Jim Crow laws*  
- *Brown v. Board of Education* ruling  
- Newspaper articles on the *murder of Emmett Till*  
- **Women’s Political Council (WPC) Leaflet** (December 2, 1955)  
- Excerpts from **Langston Hughes’ "A Little Letter to the White Citizens of the South"**  
- Arrest report of **Rosa Parks**  
- **Claudette Colvin’s statement**  

**Instructions:**  
1. **Source Breakdown:**  
   - Divide students into groups, each assigned a different document.
   - Groups analyze their document using guiding questions:  
     - What is the purpose of this document?  
     - Who is the intended audience?  
     - How does it reflect the racial and social conditions of the time?  
     - What strategies for activism and resistance are evident?  

2. **Discussion Forum:**  
   - Each group presents their findings, highlighting key takeaways.  
   - Compare and contrast perspectives, particularly regarding **women’s roles, youth activism, and legal challenges**.  

3. **Reflection Assignment:**  
   - Students write a short reflection or discussion post responding to:  
     - How did these documents shape the Civil Rights Movement?  
     - What lessons can activists today learn from these historical texts?  

4. **Debrief Session:**  
   - Class-wide discussion on the **intersection of law, media, and grassroots activism**.  
   - Debate whether **Claudette Colvin or Rosa Parks** was the most strategic choice for the movement’s public face.  

**Assessment:**  
- Participation in discussion  
- Analysis depth in group presentations  
- Reflection quality in written response  

---

This activity will help students engage deeply with primary sources while fostering critical thinking about the strategies and challenges of the Civil Rights Movement. Let me know if you’d like any adjustments!
